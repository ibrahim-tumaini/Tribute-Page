Nelson Mandela

A universal symbol of freedom and reconciliation

Here’s a clear, brief timeline of Nelson Mandela’s life:

  • 1918 – Born on 18 July in Mvezo, South Africa.
  • 1944 – Helped form the African National Congress (ANC) Youth League.
  • 1952 – Led the Defiance Campaign against apartheid laws.
  • 1962 – Arrested and later sentenced to life imprisonment for fighting apartheid.
  • 1964–1990 – Spent 27 years in prison, mostly on Robben Island.
  • 1990 – Released from prison on 11 February; continued the struggle for freedom.
  • 1993 – Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize alongside F.W. de Klerk.
  • 1994 – Elected as South Africa’s first Black president in the first democratic elections.
  • 1999 – Stepped down after one term as president.
  • 2013 – Passed away on 5 December, aged 95.
